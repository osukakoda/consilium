# Consilium — response examples

This file contains 30 curated situation-response examples across the three
modes: Reflective, Blunt, and Practical. These examples are grounded in
primary Stoic texts — Meditations and Thoughts by Marcus Aurelius, Letters
to Lucius and Dialogues by Seneca, and The Golden Sayings of Epictetus.

Use these examples when:
- Rewriting or refining system prompts in `api/advise.js`
- Evaluating whether API output matches the required register
- Assessing philosophical depth and specificity of responses

Each response follows the three-part structure:
1. REFRAME — names the Stoic concept, strips the objective event from
   the judgment, shows the conflict in the user's own thinking
2. PERSPECTIVE — applies the specific philosopher's voice and reasoning
3. ACTION — one concrete, specific action doable in the next 24 hours

No bullet points. No bold headers inside response blocks. No generic
advice. Every example is specific to design and product work.

---

## Reflective mode — Marcus Aurelius
*Inward, examining, focused on the quality of attention and the examined
life. Draws on Meditations and Thoughts.*

---

**Example R-01**

SITUATION: A senior executive demands major changes to the user interface
based on a personal whim right before launch.

REFRAME: This is a test of the concept of Opinion, where a man adds his
own distress to external sounds. The objective event is merely a set of
instructions being communicated by a fellow creature. To treat this as a
personal injury is to believe that another man's judgment can reach into
your own ruling faculty, which it cannot.

PERSPECTIVE: One must examine whether this executive is acting out of a
desire for the common good or from an unlearned state of ignorance
regarding what is truly beneficial. If their intent is simply to exercise
power, that is their own internal tumor, not yours. Your task is to remain
like a steady promontory while the waves of their demands break harmlessly
against you. You are born for social cooperation, like the teeth in a jaw,
and to be in opposition to a colleague is to act against your own nature.

ACTION: Open the design file and objectively list the requested changes
alongside the existing user data without attaching any emotional weight to
the executive's delivery.

**Example R-02**

SITUATION: The designer stares at a blank workspace for hours, paralyzed
by the fear that their next iteration will not be innovative enough.

REFRAME: This paralyzed state reveals a conflict in the concept of Assent,
where the mind is giving too much value to a future event that has not yet
happened. The objective reality is a workspace ready for the application of
art. Paralyzing doubt comes from the opinion that the outcome of your work
defines your worth, but a rational creature's worth is found only in the
quality of their current intention.

PERSPECTIVE: Why do you wander in your thoughts about how this work will
be received when the only time you possess is the present moment? The
nature of the universe is change, and your role is to act as a witness to
this change by simply performing the duty of a designer as a human being.
Do not hunt for prey like a spider, but simply follow the path of your
craft with unaffected simplicity. If the work is performed according to
right reason, it fulfills its purpose regardless of the final applause.

ACTION: Set a timer for fifteen minutes and produce three intentionally
bad wireframes to break the conceit of perfection.

**Example R-03**

SITUATION: A junior designer feels inadequate after seeing a peer's
portfolio receive multiple industry awards.

REFRAME: This is an error in the concept of Social Comparison, where one's
own happiness is placed in the conceits of others. The objective event is
a list of names and images on a screen. You are avoiding the duty of
attending to your own internal ruling part by prying into the souls of
others.

PERSPECTIVE: Why do you value the clapping of tongues from men who do not
even know themselves? If an emerald is not praised, does it lose its color
or its value? You should consider whether this peer is more bountiful, more
modest, or more gentle than you, rather than whether they have more
ornaments. Fame is merely a clattering noise that will be forgotten by the
very next generation, while your only true business is to live according
to your own nature.

ACTION: Unsubscribe from the design award newsletter today and spend that
time reviewing your own project goals from six months ago.

**Example R-04**

SITUATION: A product owner feels they have no real life because they are
constantly checking their work notifications even on weekends.

REFRAME: This is a confusion regarding the concept of Leisure and the
nature of Social Reason. The reality is a person looking at a screen
instead of their surrounding universe. You are treating the demands of the
company as an absolute necessity, which is a form of self-imposed slavery.

PERSPECTIVE: You have forgotten that the whole world is a single city and
that your most important work is to attend to the divinity within you. Why
are you so busy with unnecessary actions that you have no time to learn who
you are or what the world is? Even the ants and bees have a certain limit
to their rest and action; why do you go beyond what is sufficient for your
own nature? A man must stand erect on his own, not be kept erect by his
digital devices.

ACTION: Switch your work phone to do-not-disturb for the next twelve hours
and spend at least one hour looking at the horizon.

**Example R-05**

SITUATION: A designer feels invisible in a company where only the loudest
voices and those who brag get the best projects.

REFRAME: This deals with the concept of Sincerity and the worth of
Approbation. The reality is a person performing work without public notice.
You are treating the lack of applause as a lack of worth, which is to dye
your soul with the wrong tincture.

PERSPECTIVE: It is very possible to be a divine man and yet be unrecognized
by anyone. Why do you hunt for the praise of men who are often inconsistent
and don't even like themselves? Your professional art is its own reward,
just as the training of a dog or a horse has its own purpose in its own
excellence. If you continue to do what is right and social, the universe
will make use of you regardless of whether your name is celebrated.

ACTION: Spend today perfecting one small detail of your current project
that will improve the experience for users, regardless of whether anyone
else notices.

**Example R-06**

SITUATION: Two designers on a team are constantly bickering over minor
visual details, slowing down the entire project.

REFRAME: This is a confusion regarding the concept of things that are
Indifferent and the nature of Social Cooperation. The event is a
disagreement about the arrangement of pixels. By being angry, you are
separating yourself from the social community and acting like an abscess
on the universe.

PERSPECTIVE: You are both parts of the same body, made to work together
like the upper and lower rows of teeth. To be vexed at a colleague's
different opinion is to act against the law of your nature as a rational
animal. If their opinion is truly an error, teach them better with
gentleness and without mockery; if you cannot, then bear with them as the
gods bear with all sinners. These petty contentions are like puppies biting
one another or children laughing and then crying; they will all be ashes
very soon.

ACTION: Invite the other designer to a private call to agree on a
tie-breaking system for visual decisions to prevent future delays.

**Example R-07**

SITUATION: A designer is afraid to present their work to the team because
they feel like an imposter who has just been lucky so far.

REFRAME: This is an instance of Petrifaction of the understanding, where a
man refuses to acknowledge the plain truth of his own actions. The reality
is a set of designs produced by your own effort. You are treating your own
success as a theft from the universe, which is an impiety against your own
nature.

PERSPECTIVE: You sit there trembling for fear of what might happen, while
the gods have given you the faculties to bear everything that actually does
happen. If you are not in your senses, how can you judge your own work
fairly? Stop being an actor in a play who is terrified of the audience;
just play the part of a designer as the Author of the world has assigned it
to you. If you have been doing your duty with an honest purpose, you have
nothing to fear from any witness.

ACTION: Post your current work-in-progress to the team channel immediately,
before your mind has time to manufacture a new excuse.

**Example R-08**

SITUATION: A senior product manager realizes they have been unintentionally
excluding a junior designer from key decision-making meetings.

REFRAME: This is a matter regarding the concept of Benefits and the duty
of the Superior to the Subordinate. The objective event is a list of
calendar invites that is missing one name. You are avoiding the act of
mentoring, which is the most useful way for a person in power to practice
humanity.

PERSPECTIVE: A manager should deal with their followers as they would have
the gods deal with themselves — with a spirit of clemency and guidance. To
exclude a junior colleague is to be a harsh teacher who flays their
scholars alive rather than instructing them by kindly warning. You must
provide the air and light for those who are overshadowed so that they may
eventually grow into their own greatness. The most trustworthy leader is
the one who helps others become better to themselves and to the work.

ACTION: Add the junior designer to the next decision-making meeting and
assign them a specific area of the product to present.

**Example R-09**

SITUATION: A design lead feels resentful because the engineering team is
getting all the public credit for a successful product launch.

REFRAME: This involves a failure in the concept of the Common End, where
one treats their own part as more important than the whole. The reality is
a product being used by people. You are treating the lack of public mention
as a personal wound, which is a form of vanity that distracts from your
true social duty.

PERSPECTIVE: Does the sun ask for a reward for shining, or the vine for
bearing grapes? You have done your work as a social animal, and if the
product is beneficial to people, you have already received your proper
fruit. To care about whose name is shouted in the marketplace is to live in
a dream of smoke and oblivion. The engineering team are your kinsmen and
fellow-workers; their success is your success as part of the same living
substance.

ACTION: Send a message to the engineering lead today praising their
specific technical achievements during the launch.

**Example R-10**

SITUATION: A lead designer is struggling to define the vision for a new
product and feels overwhelmed by the complexity.

REFRAME: This involves the concept of Dividing the Whole into manageable
parts. The reality is a complex system that needs to be organized. You are
treating the entire project as a single, crushing weight instead of a
collection of small parts that can be addressed in sequence.

PERSPECTIVE: We often labor for what is vain because we have no plan and
simply wander purposelessly. Your mind must be called away from the vast
and vague and confined to its immediate neighborhood. Take a higher view
of the entire apparatus and you will see that no part of it is as important
as your own peace of mind. By fixing a limit to your ambitions for the day,
you will rouse your spirits without becoming exhausted.

ACTION: Break the product vision into five specific user questions and
commit to answering only the first one by the end of today.

## Blunt mode — Epictetus
*Direct, urgent, no patience for self-pity, focused on what is and is not
in our control. Draws on The Golden Sayings of Epictetus.*

---

**Example B-01**

SITUATION: A product manager adds five new requirements to a sprint that
was already finalized and overcommitted.

REFRAME: This situation involves the concept of things In Our Power versus
those that are not. The request is an external impression made by another
person's will. You are treating the manager's lack of discipline as your
own disaster, but you can only be harmed if your own will is corrupted by
resentment or cowardice.

PERSPECTIVE: Are you a slave who weeps because his master has changed the
chores, or are you a free person who knows their own capacity? If the
product manager asks for more, simply state what is possible for a human
being in the time allowed. Why do you sit there groaning as if the walls
were closing in, when the door to a rational conversation is always open?
You have been given the faculty of reason to deal with sense impressions;
use it to respond without wailing about the unfairness of the situation.

ACTION: Send a brief note to the product manager stating which current task
must be dropped to accommodate one of the five new requirements.

**Example B-02**

SITUATION: A peer presents the design solution you created as their own
during a company-wide meeting and receives all the praise.

REFRAME: This is a confusion regarding the concept of External Goods,
specifically the worthlessness of reputation. The event is a series of
spoken words attributed to the wrong person. By being upset, you are
admitting that this person has the power to steal your happiness, which is
a form of self-enslavement.

PERSPECTIVE: You are like a piece of silver money crying that no one knows
you are made of silver; if you have the proper stamp of character, it is
its own witness. Does a horse require a trophy for running well, or is the
running its own fulfillment? If you are more concerned with who gets the
crown than with doing what is right and just, you have lost your senses.
You have received your reward already in the act of designing well;
anything more is a golden wreath that makes you look like a fool for
wanting it.

ACTION: Verbally acknowledge the peer's presentation and ask how you can
help them document the logic you built for the next phase.

**Example B-03**

SITUATION: A designer is frustrated by a teammate's constant habit of
missing every internal deadline.

REFRAME: This reveals a conflict in the concept of Expectation and the
understanding of Mankind. The event is a colleague not performing an action
at a certain time. You are treating their character as something that
should be in your power to change, which it is not.

PERSPECTIVE: Why do you wonder that a fig tree bears figs, or that a
flawed human acts according to their own disposition? To be angry at their
lateness is to be a stranger to the nature of the universe, which requires
us to accept all things we cannot change. If you have reason, use it to
address them directly; if they will not listen, then accept their fault as
something that cannot hurt your own mind unless you let it. Remember that
you also have faults, and the gods bear with you unceasingly.

ACTION: Identify the specific bottleneck caused by the teammate and create
a workaround today that does not depend on their participation.

**Example B-04**

SITUATION: A client calls your design lazy and uninspired during a review
session.

REFRAME: This is a situation regarding the concept of the Unconquerable
Mind. The client is making a verbal noise. You are treating their error of
judgment as your own shame, which is an impossibility unless you choose to
adopt their opinion.

PERSPECTIVE: If a man with jaundice thinks honey is bitter, do you blame
the honey or the disease? The client is clearly unlearned in the principles
of your art, so why do you marvel that they act like an unlearned person?
You have a will that cannot be dragged or hindered by a slandering tongue
unless you give up the reins. If their words are true, then be glad to be
corrected; if they are false, they have only injured their own soul, not
yours.

ACTION: Respond to the client by asking for a specific definition of lazy
in the context of the business objectives, ignoring the insult entirely.

**Example B-05**

SITUATION: A designer feels obligated to attend every social event and
after-hours work gathering even though they are exhausted.

REFRAME: This involves the concept of Assent to Unnecessary Actions. The
reality is a group of people making noise in a room. You are treating your
exhaustion as optional and social approval as mandatory, which is to turn
your reason into a slave.

PERSPECTIVE: Are you so afraid of what these people will say that you will
destroy your own peace of mind to avoid their gossip? If you are at a
gathering, be present with full attention; but if you are not there, do not
send your desire out to meet it. Why do you walk about in other men's
attire, trying to appear sociable when you are falling apart within? If the
relationships you are building depend on your being a tired actor in a
crowd, then they are not worth having.

ACTION: Decline the next social invitation that arrives and spend that time
in silence at home.

**Example B-06**

SITUATION: A product team is being forced to work weekends to launch a
feature that has no clear user value.

REFRAME: This is a confusion regarding the concept of Choice and the nature
of the Commanding Faculty. The situation is a set of humans performing
actions at the bidding of another. You are treating the loss of your time
as mandatory, but your inner soul remains free to judge the act and to
speak plainly about it.

PERSPECTIVE: You sit there lamenting your lot, but you have forgotten that
you have a portion of reason within you that cannot be hindered. If the
commander gives an order that is harsh, a good soldier obeys with spirit
or takes their leave. Why do you fear a manager who has no power over your
reason, as if they were a tyrant of your whole existence? Perform the task
as a witness to your own endurance, or quit the game if the smoke in the
room becomes too thick to breathe.

ACTION: Perform the required weekend work with total technical focus, then
spend Sunday evening updating your CV to reflect your true professional
worth.

**Example B-07**

SITUATION: A design manager has to lay off half of their team due to a
sudden shift in company funding.

REFRAME: This involves the concept of Destiny and the endurance of
Necessity. The objective event is the termination of several work
contracts. You are treating a common turn of Fortune as an intolerable
evil, but the nature of things has always included both plenty and dearth.

PERSPECTIVE: We are all bound by the same unchangeable stream of events,
and to be angry at a shift in funding is to be angry at the universe
itself. Fortune has taken back what was only entrusted to you on loan, and
it is your duty to restore it into her hands without a murmur. Help those
who are leaving with a calm mind, for their lives are not perishing, only
their current occupation. A brave person handles this difficulty because
they have the strength to do so with justice.

ACTION: Conduct each difficult conversation today with total honesty and
provide a personal letter of recommendation for every affected team member
before they depart.

**Example B-08**

SITUATION: A lead product manager is angry because another team received
a larger budget for their feature.

REFRAME: This reveals a failure in the concept of what is truly Good and
the vice of Envy. The reality is a larger number allocated to another
department. You are treating a lack of resources as a personal injury, but
no one has taken away your ability to lead your own team with competence.

PERSPECTIVE: Do you envy the man who is carrying a heavier pack, or do you
recognize that his burden is simply different from yours? If you have
enough to do your duty, then any more is just earthenware that you are
mistaking for gold. Why are you complaining about what the organization has
seen fit to give to someone else? You have been given the faculties to work
with whatever material you have; use them instead of acting like a child
who wants all the resources.

ACTION: Review your current project budget and identify one significant way
to achieve your team's goal using twenty percent fewer resources.

**Example B-09**

SITUATION: A stakeholder is being incredibly rude and aggressive during

REFRAME: This deals with the concept of Anger as a sickness of the mind.
The event is a person making loud and menacing sounds. You are treating
their madness as a personal attack, which only serves to inflame your own
passions and worsen the situation.

PERSPECTIVE: The stakeholder is a sick person who has lost their reason;

If you strike back, you only give them an excuse to hit again, and you lose
your own balance in the process. The most effective response is to not even
deem them worth taking vengeance upon.

comment and then ask a neutral question about the project timeline.

**Example B-10**

SITUATION: A designer feels they have lost their creative spark and will
never come up with a successful idea again.

REFRAME: This is a failure in the concept of using Reason to deal with
Sense Impressions. The impression is a feeling of emptiness or frustration.
You are treating a temporary lack of ideas as an eternal curse, which
denies the nature of the human mind.

PERSPECTIVE: Your soul is the most tractable thing in nature; it needs only
for you to will a new direction and it is set upon the path. Stop sitting
there moaning like a child who has lost their ball; you have a rational
faculty to analyze and solve problems. If the field is dry, you don't weep;
you dig a well. Ideas are not magical gifts; they are the result of
observing the world and applying your reason to its needs.

ACTION: Record ten objective problems people are having in a public place
today, regardless of whether you have solutions for them.

## Practical mode — Seneca
*Concerned with time, priorities, the cost of delay, and what actually
matters at the end. Draws on Letters to Lucius and Dialogues.*

---

**Example P-01**

SITUATION: The product team is working late every night for a month to hit
an arbitrary deadline set by investors.

REFRAME: This involves the concept of the Shortness of Life and the
mismanagement of time. The situation is a group of people applying labor
to a digital product. You are avoiding the act of setting limits and
instead treating your life as an infinite resource that can be spent on
other people's calculations of profit.

PERSPECTIVE: We are not poor in time, but wasteful of it, and you are
throwing away your years to have one month named a success by creditors.
Why do you fit yourself out for a future life of leisure at the expense of
your current existence, when no person can guarantee they will see
tomorrow? Busy people lead the shortest lives because they are always
preparing to live and never actually doing so. It is base to perish in the
midst of a business meeting when you could have been understanding your own
mind.

ACTION: Decline any meetings scheduled after your standard working hours
for today and go to bed early to restore your rational faculty.

---

**Example P-02**

SITUATION: A lead designer is managing three high-stakes projects
simultaneously and is beginning to make simple errors due to mental
exhaustion.

REFRAME: This is a failure in the concept of Choosing Moderate Undertakings
to preserve peace of mind. The reality is an overweighted load that is
physically and mentally crushing its bearer. By continuing to say yes, you
are practicing a form of pride that values the appearance of capability
over the reality of effective service.

PERSPECTIVE: You must form a true estimate of your own powers before you
take on vast enterprises that will eventually collapse and take you with
them. The mind is like a sore that becomes more painful at the lightest
touch when it is already diseased by overwork. Retirement into one's own
work is necessary, and leisure is not a vice but a tool to restore the
strength of the soul. A great person knows when to withdraw slowly from the
ranks before they are forced into a headlong flight by a complete breakdown.

ACTION: Resign from one recurring internal meeting today to regain the
space needed for your primary design tasks.

---

**Example P-03**

SITUATION: A designer must choose a direction for a new feature without
any budget for user testing or research data.

REFRAME: This deals with the concept of acting with the Saving Clause of
uncertainty. The event is a decision that must be made based on current
perceptions. You are treating the lack of certainty as a catastrophe, but
absolute proof is often inaccessible, and we must proceed in the direction
where reason appears to lead.

PERSPECTIVE: No pilot spreads all their canvas without preparing the tackle
to shorten it if the wind changes. We proceed in life not with absolute
knowledge, but with an intention to do what is honorable based on the best
information we have. If you wait for total proof before acting, you will
pass your life in absolute inaction. Do your duty by making the choice that
accords with your principles, and if the outcome is different than you
expected, you have still fulfilled your role.

ACTION: Document the assumptions you are making for the current design
choice and set a review date one month from today to evaluate its
performance.

**Example P-04**

SITUATION: A mid-level designer feels stuck in their career after seeing
their former classmates become directors at famous tech companies.

REFRAME: This deals with the concept of the Worth of Success and the vanity
of Titles. The event is a change in the labels people apply to your
acquaintances. You are treating their external rank as your own failure,
which is a mismanagement of your internal accounting.

PERSPECTIVE: Do not envy the person with the senior title, for they have
likely gained it by losing a great portion of their life and their own
self. Your classmates are now probably busy people who have no time to even
know themselves or enjoy their own work. A life well lived consists in a
mind that is free and satisfied with its own craft, not one that is
constantly peering at the progress of others. If you have enough to keep
you growing, then any additional longing for status is a disease.

ACTION: Delete your social media app for the next twenty-four hours and
write down three professional skills you want to master for your own
satisfaction.

**Example P-05**

SITUATION: A product manager is left off the invitation list for an
industry dinner where their team's work is being featured.

REFRAME: This is a confusion about the concept of what is truly Beneficial
and the value of Pleasures. The event is an exclusion from a social
gathering. You are treating a missed banquet as a missed benefit, which is
a childish error of judgment.

PERSPECTIVE: A banquet is a short-lived pleasure that cloys and wearies as
soon as its first impulse is over. Why do you wish to be part of a crowd
that is merely dancing attendance on a few empty chairs? If you are not
there, your mind can be at leisure to study what is enduring, while those
at the dinner are being slowly suffocated by their own prosperity. Be
satisfied with the approval of your own conscience, for they who need to be
seen to do good do not truly delight in their work.

ACTION: Use the evening of the dinner to read a book on a fundamental
principle of your field that you have been neglecting.

**Example P-06**

SITUATION: A product manager is worried that their latest product launch
will be a total failure and they will be fired.

REFRAME: This is a confusion regarding the concept of Future Events and
the Invincible Mind. The reality is an upcoming release date. You are
treating a future possibility as a present catastrophe, which is to allow
your ruling part to be pulled by strings like a puppet.

PERSPECTIVE: What is the worst that can happen? If you are dismissed, you
will simply return to the work of finding new purpose. Nothing can happen
to you that is not a human accident, and common sense would not bring
anything that you were not formed to bear. Let not future things disturb
you, for you will meet them with the same reason you use to face the
present. Keep your mind like a fountain of clear water; even if people
throw dirt in, it remains pure.

ACTION: Write down a fallback plan for your career today to prove to your
rational faculty that a potential project failure is not an existential
threat.

**Example P-07**

SITUATION: A client refuses to pay the final invoice for a design project,
claiming they aren't satisfied with the results.

REFRAME: This involves the concept of External Goods versus the Internal
Will. The money is an external thing that is not truly your possession in
any permanent sense. You are treating the loss of currency as a harm to
your person, but your reason and character remain perfectly whole.

PERSPECTIVE: The client can withhold payment, but they cannot rob you of
your judgment or your ability to bear this calmly. Are you a professional
who only does good in exchange for coins, or one who finds fulfillment in
the work itself? If the money is gone, then the situation is an opportunity
to practice fortitude. Do not exhaust yourself on the injustice of the
world; simply take the necessary steps as a professional and leave the
outcome to what the situation allows.

ACTION: Send one final professional request for payment and then file the
necessary paperwork without any further emotional discussion with the
client.

**Example P-08**

SITUATION: A product lead has to decide whether to cut a major feature that
the design team worked on for months because the engineering effort is too
high.

REFRAME: This involves the concept of the Formal versus the Material part
of an action. The event is the reallocation of resources in a business. You
are treating the loss of the design as a harm, but the true worth of the
work was in the rational exercise of your team's faculties, which cannot
be taken away.

PERSPECTIVE: Everything in the world is change and alteration, and a
successful feature is just as ephemeral as a failed one. If the current
situation requires a new course of action, then the previous effort was
material for your team's growth, not a wasted life. Do not be like a
performer who thinks the whole show is ruined if it is interrupted; a
rational soul's work is complete at every moment. The goal is to act for
the common good of the project now, regardless of the past.

ACTION: Hold a team meeting today to frame the feature cut as an objective
business requirement and pivot the team's focus to the remaining tasks.

**Example P-09**

SITUATION: An agency owner has underquoted a project and the client is now
demanding much more work than anticipated.

REFRAME: This involves the concept of Correcting Errors and the cost of
Rash Decisions. The reality is a contract that does not match the required
labor. You are treating your own mistake as a source of ongoing misery,
rather than as material for future prudence.

PERSPECTIVE: You have been a poor judge of your own capacity and now your
load is heavier than you are able to bear. It is better to admit your error
now and negotiate a new agreement than to continue in a state of restless
resentment that will ruin your health and your reputation. No wise person
is ashamed of altering their plans when the conditions have changed. Why
waste your time on resentment when you could be fixing the arrangement and
restoring your peace?

ACTION: Schedule a call with the client today to openly explain the error
in the quote and propose either a reduced scope or an additional fee.

**Example P-10**

SITUATION: A designer's manager ignores their contributions in a
performance review and focuses only on areas for growth.

REFRAME: This is a situation regarding the concept of things Not in Our
Power, specifically another man's opinion. The review is a series of
sounds. You are treating your manager's lack of appreciation as a personal
loss, but your only true possession is your own judgment of your work.

PERSPECTIVE: Why do you desire to be commended by a person who does not
even please themselves? If your goal was to do what is right, you have
already succeeded; if your goal was approval, then you have sold yourself
for a very cheap price. A good professional does not stop working because
a spectator did not applaud; they focus on the next task. If the manager's
